Are You Meeting Your Office Attendance Requirements?

As hybrid work policies tighten across industries, office attendance compliance has become a genuine concern for millions of workers. Roughly 69% of companies with hybrid arrangements now actively track and measure whether employees are meeting their in-office requirements. And for many workers, the consequences of falling short are real: performance review impacts, loss of remote work privileges, or even being flagged for disciplinary conversations.

The challenge is that attendance thresholds vary widely. Some companies require 40% office attendance, which translates to about two days per week. Others set the bar at 60% or even 80%, meaning three to four mandatory in-office days. Understanding exactly where you stand relative to your company's specific threshold can be the difference between a smooth performance review and an uncomfortable one.

There is another important reason to track your own attendance percentage: HR systems are not always accurate. Badge swipe data can miss days when you tailgate through a door, arrive before the system activates, or work from a different floor or building. Some companies rely on WiFi connection logs, which can be equally unreliable. By keeping your own attendance records, you have a backup if the official numbers do not match your actual presence.

This attendance compliance calculator gives you a quick monthly check-in. Enter your total workdays and the days you went into the office, and optionally set your company's required percentage to see whether you are on track. It takes ten seconds to use, and doing it at the end of each month ensures you are never caught off guard. If you are falling behind, you will have time to course-correct before anyone else notices.
